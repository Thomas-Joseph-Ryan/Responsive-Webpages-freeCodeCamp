Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug holding wheat crops farmed in mexico that were genetically modified to fight starvation

Timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - 1944 - Character development
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grow wheat twice each season, doubling the yield
  • 1970 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - Helps seven african counties dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields

"Borlaugs life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that ones man towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress" -- Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
